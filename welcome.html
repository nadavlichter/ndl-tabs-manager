Custom groups help you organize your tabs automatically based on your needs. Here's how to use them:

What are Custom Groups?

Custom groups automatically collect related tabs based on keywords you choose. Here are some examples:

Work Group

Keywords: jira, confluence, github, gitlab, docs.google

All your work-related tabs will automatically group together!

Shopping Group

Keywords: amazon, ebay, etsy, walmart

Keep your shopping tabs organized in one place.

Social Media

Keywords: facebook, twitter, instagram, linkedin

All social media tabs grouped together.

How to Create a Custom Group:

  1. Click the TabToolKit icon in your browser toolbar
  2. Click Manage Groups at the bottom of the popup
  3. Click + Add New Group
  4. Fill in:
    • Group Name: What you want to call your group (e.g., "Work")
    • Keywords: Words found in URLs that should go in this group (e.g., "github, jira")
    • Color: Pick a color to easily identify your group
  5. Click Save
Pro Tips:
  • Keywords are not case-sensitive - "GitHub" and "github" work the same
  • Use partial words - "mail" will match "gmail" and "outlook.mail"
  • Separate keywords with commas
  • The more specific your keywords, the better the grouping
